Clinical summary (under construction): This meta-analysis suggests that the clinical intervention is effective at reducing duration of antibiotics; however, meta-regression indicates that the benefit inversely correlates independently with the duration of antibiotics in the control group. The heterogeneity was partly by this factor (see meta-regression below) yielding heterogeneity that was still 'substantial' (residual I2 = 78%; 95% CI: 44% to 95%). The quality of evidence is pending due to quality of trials and small number of subjects studied. We included randomized controlled trials that compared procalcitonin-guided antibiotics versus usual care or alternative stratregies that the meta-analysis below (1) identified and additional trial(s) published subsequently. Additional interpretations may be in the references in the 'Cited by' section below.
